# Departments

## General and Organization Department

!!! info inline end ""

    <figure markdown="span" style="max-width: 300px;">
    ![General and Organization Department](gene.jpg){ loading=lazy }
    <figcaption>General and Organization Department</figcaption>
    </figure>

The General and Organization Department serves as the team's coordination hub, responsible for daily operations, resource coordination, and project progress management. Department members formulate work plans, coordinate cross-departmental collaboration, manage project resources, and ensure efficient team operations. Additionally, they handle external liaison and documentation management, providing important support for the team to achieve its objectives.

## Theory and Exploration Department

!!! info inline end ""

    <figure markdown="span" style="max-width: 300px;">
    ![Theory and Exploration Department](theo.jpg){ loading=lazy }
    <figcaption>Theory and Exploration Department</figcaption>
    </figure>

The Theory and Exploration Department is the team's technical think tank, focusing on cutting-edge technology research and theoretical model construction. Department members conduct feasibility studies, technical solution design, simulation, and theoretical verification. Through in-depth exploration of aerospace, intelligent systems, optical computing, and other fields, they provide a solid theoretical foundation and innovative direction for team projects.

## Chemistry and Materials Department

!!! info inline end ""

    <figure markdown="span" style="max-width: 300px;">
    ![Chemistry and Materials Department](chem.jpg){ loading=lazy }
    <figcaption>Chemistry and Materials Department</figcaption>
    </figure>

The Chemistry and Materials Department is responsible for propellant formulation research, material testing, and performance optimization. Department members focus on fuel chemistry research, material heat resistance testing, and propellant performance parameter optimization, ensuring safe and reliable operation of rocket engine systems. They also conduct experiments with new materials, exploring more efficient and environmentally friendly propulsion solutions, providing critical material support for aerospace engineering.

## Electronics and Information Department

!!! info inline end ""

    <figure markdown="span" style="max-width: 300px;">
    ![Electronics and Information Department](elect.jpg){ loading=lazy }
    <figcaption>Electronics and Information Department</figcaption>
    </figure>

The Electronics and Information Department is the technical core of the team, responsible for electronic system design, embedded development, and information system construction. Department members specialize in flight control systems, sensor networks, data acquisition, and processing platform development, ensuring precise control of rocket projects and stable operation of smart campus systems. Through continuous technological innovation, they provide reliable electronic and information technology solutions for the team.

## Engineering and Assembly Department

!!! info inline end ""

    <figure markdown="span" style="max-width: 300px;">
    ![Engineering and Assembly Department](engin.jpg){ loading=lazy }
    <figcaption>Engineering and Assembly Department</figcaption>
    </figure>

The Engineering and Assembly Department is responsible for transforming design concepts into tangible results, undertaking mechanical design, component manufacturing, and system integration. Department members handle rocket structure design, 3D printing manufacturing, precision component machining, and overall assembly and testing, ensuring high-quality implementation of design solutions. Through strict engineering standards and exquisite craftsmanship, they provide reliable technical support for team projects.

## Ethics and Oversight Committee

!!! info inline end ""

    <figure markdown="span" style="max-width: 300px;">
    ![Ethics and Oversight Committee](eoc.jpg){ loading=lazy }
    <figcaption>Ethics and Oversight Committee</figcaption>
    </figure>

The Ethics and Oversight Committee serves as the team's guardian of quality and safety, responsible for ensuring projects comply with scientific ethics, safety standards, and engineering specifications. Committee members establish operational protocols, supervise experimental safety, and assess project risks, ensuring rocket launches, chemical experiments, and other activities proceed within safe and controllable boundaries. Through strict quality control and ethical supervision, they guarantee team member safety and project compliance.
